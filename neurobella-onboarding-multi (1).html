03 — The System

The 5 Concept Levels

Every brief is tagged with a concept level. You always start at Level 2. Then, as learnings come in, you move into Levels 3, 4 and 5 on the winners. Level 1 is not in your scope — net-new concepting is handled by the founder/team.

1

Net New Concepts 🔒 Not your scope

Completely new angles or mechanisms unseen in the ad account (sourced from Kalodata, organic, competitors, etc.). Owned by the founder/team — not part of your weekly output.

2

New Structural Formats ▶ Start here

Same mechanism / angle / desire, delivered in a different script format or narrative style — e.g. testimonial-driven, UGC storyline, advertorial, etc. This is your default entry point for every avatar. Go to AdSpy to spy on winning creatives and source the new structural formats worth modeling.

3

Major Variations on Winners After learnings

Same core idea, adjusted for a new symptom, desire, avatar or tone — e.g. pain relief → mobility, AI voice → doctor, swapped mechanism.

4

Lead / Stitch Variations After learnings

Variations on winning concepts — creating "Level 4" lead / stitch variations on the winners.

5

Hook Variations After learnings

Variations on winning concepts — creating "Level 5" hook variations to keep fresh creative running and fight fatigue while scaling.

05 — How You Work

Your weekly workflow

Follow this every cycle, for both angles. The research gate is non-negotiable — nothing gets briefed until your research is approved.

1

Deep-dive: avatar & angle research

Start with the avatar and the angle. Angle = the condition we target (macular degeneration, diabetic retinopathy). Do real voice-of-customer research to learn how the customer thinks — manual digging plus Claude.

  • Amazon reviews — the exact language, pains and desires
  • Reddit — communities around the angle/condition
  • Facebook groups — for the angles we're running
  • Use Claude to organize and pressure-test your findings
✓

Research approval gate

Send your research over for review before any briefs are written. Once it's signed off, you're cleared to build.

⛔ No briefs until research is approved
2

Mine winning creative

Go to AdSpy, the Meta Ad Library, Kalodata and the competitor tools we give you. Pull proven structures worth modeling — for both native ads and videos — fuel for your Level 2 reworks.

3

Write the 30 briefs

Per angle: 10 native ads + 5 videos. Choose the format (advertorial/native or VSL, UGC, AI, etc.), match each to the right awareness level, tag the concept level (start at 2), and write clean DR copy: hook → mechanism → proof → offer → CTA. Target ≥50% hook / ≥25% hold.

4

Log everything in the Master Sheet

Add every brief into the Creative Master Sheet with its angle, avatar, pain, mechanism, format and concept level. Stay ruthlessly organized — the sheet is the system.

5

Create the Notion card

After the sheet is locked, create a card on the Notion board using the exact naming convention — so when it's live in Meta you instantly know which concept it is.

[Batch] · [Concept name] · [Angle] · [Awareness level] · [Format code] · [Goal]
B12 · Macular-Starvation · Macular-Degeneration · Problem-Aware · Advertorial · Beat-control
6

Direct the editors

Editors pick up their tasks in Notion (not the Master Sheet). Direct them clearly so each brief becomes a strong native ad or video. You own the creative vision through to delivery.

7

Feedback loop & learnings (manual)

Once the batch is live, let the creatives run ~7 days, then go through the data manually to see why the batch is winning or losing. Those learnings decide the next batch — you don't guess, you build on what the data says.

  • Ask the media buyer for the data, or
  • Check Adnova creative analytics, or
  • Pull it from Meta Ads Manager if you have access
Winners → Level 3, 4 & 5 variations · Losers → cut & document why

Weekly output: 10 native ads + 5 videos
Avatar 1 — “The Newly Diagnosed” · dodge the needle

A 59-year-old who's lived with type 2 diabetes for more than a decade, just told at a routine eye screening that the doctor now sees “the first changes” — microaneurysms and tiny dot-like bleeds, the earliest warning signs of diabetic retinopathy. They're at the very start of the disease and terrified of where it leads — because they've already watched where it leads: a mother who also had diabetes, endured years of eye injections (Eylea, Lucentis) and laser, lost her independence one appointment at a time, and went blind anyway. They carry the gnawing dread that they're walking their mother's exact path — and underneath it, the guilt that maybe they did this to themselves, even though they've been trying. Skeptical of supplements after wasting money on drugstore eye vitamins that did nothing, enraged at a profit-driven medical system that shuffles them through “step-therapy,” bills $2,000 a needle, and treats them like a number on a conveyor belt. They are fiercely determined to dodge the needle, refuse to believe injections are the only road, and find a real, root-cause alternative before it's too late.

🔥 Their Desire

“I am NOT going on injections for the rest of my life. I'm not following my mother's path. There has to be another way.”

“I don't want to go blind and stick needles in my eyes. I want 20/20 clear vision like I did in my 20s.”

Avatar 2 — “The Treadmill Veteran” · already on the shots, wants more

A 68-year-old who's lived with type 2 diabetes for nearly twenty years and has had diagnosed diabetic retinopathy for the last four. They're not at the “early changes” stage anymore — they're in the loop: anti-VEGF injections (Eylea, then Lucentis) every several weeks, a round of laser already behind them. They know the cold metal clamp, the needle coming down, the days afterward with a bloodshot, blood-pooled eye and a gritty ache, the dark “little dementors” of floaters that drift across everything. They've stopped driving at night because the headlights bloom into blinding starbursts, and they've started holding the newspaper at arm's length under a brighter and brighter lamp. And the cruelest part: they do everything right — a textbook A1C — and still hear “there's new leaking” at nearly every visit. They're exhausted by it, the bone-deep 24/7/365 burnout, and underneath the exhaustion is the fear they never say out loud: that this only moves one direction, that they're not stopping it, just managing how fast they go blind. They've already wasted money on drugstore eye vitamins that did nothing. They're not looking to quit their treatment — they're terrified to. They're looking for the thing no one in that 15-minute appointment ever gave them: something they can do themselves, between the shots, to hold onto the sight they have left.

🔥 Their Desire

“I'm already on the injections — and my eyes keep getting worse anyway. I refuse to just sit in that chair every few weeks and watch myself go blind. There has to be more I can do than wait between shots.”

Unique Mechanism — Problem / Root Cause · "The A1C Trap → Two Battlefields"

Your A1C measures the sugar in your blood. But the damage to your eyes isn't happening in your blood — it's happening inside the eye itself, behind a wall your blood sugar simply can't reach. That's why you can do everything right, bring your A1C down to a perfect 5.7 — and still sit in the chair and hear “there's new leaking.” It feels like a punishment. It feels like you failed. You didn't.

The one thing no one explained: there are two battlefields. Your insulin, pills, diet and finger-sticks all fight on the first one — your bloodstream. But the leaking, swelling and bleeding happen on the second battlefield, deep inside the eye, where nothing in your current treatment was ever built to go. You haven't been losing. You've been fighting in the wrong place.

Hero pain: plant the A1C betrayal in agitation → pay it off with the two-battlefields reveal
